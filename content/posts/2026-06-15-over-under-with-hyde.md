Hyde over at Lazy Bears interviewed me for Over/Under #70, part of #100DaysToOffload. Reposting it here for my own audience. The original lives on [lazybea.rs](https://lazybea.rs/ovr-070/).

This week my guest is Ed Leeman. From his about page, Ed is a Software Engineer, Self Hoster, DevOpser, Photographer, and Dad. He has been writing many posts about self hosting, Docker, Pi-hole and many other DevOps things.

## Podman

**Underrated!**

There is no excuse in this day and age to not use Podman. Straight swap from Docker. Same commands, same functionality. Even Docker Compose is compatible. Add an alias for Docker if you really need to. But most importantly, rootless. And none of the big tech shenanigans.

## Thunderbird

**Underrated.**

I daily Thunderbird on all my Linux devices. Mostly out of necessity. I have many mail accounts which all ingest email. Having them in Thunderbird just makes management simpler. Not to mention the more advanced features like PGP integration and plugins like DKIM / SPF checkers for determining spam.

Be great if there was an iOS version, I'm having to run my own PWA for the same functionality on mobile. I've used Thunderbird for literal YEARS, I have nostalgic attachments to it. It feels like only really in the last year or so, has the design actually changed! It takes me back to the peak of ignorance phase of the Dunning-Kruger Effect as a teenager exploring the web, learning to build the web and how to host the web.

## Cloud

**Overrated.**

This one I couldn't decide on. I'm a self-proclaimed privacy advocate, I consider myself as the product whenever using something 'free'. I've hosted my own infra, given big tech the middle finger yadda yadda. But at some point in my life - probably since having children now that I'm thinking about it - hosting my own just isn't feasible. The most expensive thing I have is time. And when you have young children, that fee is 10x. Time I will never get back, which I can't afford to spend maintaining a homelab.

I'll probably get a bunch of flak for this, but I still try to make sensible choices when favouring convenience: Encrypt where I can (so that I'm not the product). Ensure I have an exit strategy (data exports). Can I pay someone else to host it for me with OSS alternatives?

Maybe once the kids are older they might show an interest. But I get enough excitement from $WORK in Platform Engineering to keep up with the infrastructure side of things.

So to sum up. Overrated or Underrated - somewhere in between I think. If you blindly trust the Cloud, I envy you. Sometimes convenience wins, but do what you can to not be the product.

## Ubuntu

**Underrated.**

Ubuntu is great. I've distro-hopped since I was 11. My first server was Linux Mint. Then Ubuntu, PopOS, Arch btw, Manjaro and back to Ubuntu. It just works. As I said in the Cloud section, I'm at a point in my life where things just need to work. Arch is fun, I run Arch on my main laptop. But that's my play time. If I need something to work, it's always Ubuntu Server.

## Cucumbers

**Underrated**

Uhh, Hyde? You good man?

Well, Cucumbers are incredibly versatile. Not only are they a staple in my childrens' lunchboxes. But they can also be a life saver when you're in trouble. You can whittle one into an emergency spoon. You can whittle one into pretty much anything to be honest. Give yourself enough time and you could probably clone a key.

## And my replies ⤵️

### Podman

**Underrated.**

Totally agree with Ed for all the points mentioned. It just works!

### Thunderbird

I haven't used it for probably more than 15 years ago. And, even back then, mutt was my primary mua.

My terminal, tmux is all I need to be honest. So, to me it would be probably overrated, but it's still a great product for many users, and, for them I would say underrated.

### Cloud

**Overrated!**

The Cloud is just someone's else computer. I've been selfhosting for … over two decades now: email, websites, and other services. I don't trust big tech, and I disagree a bit with Ed.

It's not that you don't have time, it's just not a priority.

As a father, I'm even more concerned, and I don't want to rely on anyone for the family pictures, calendar & co.

### Ubuntu

**Overrated!**

I tried it years ago, and it lasted an afternoon. I've been always using, and installing for people a pure Debian. It just works!

Recently, I've read a couple of toots where people got annoyed by Ubuntu too.
